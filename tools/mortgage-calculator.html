Understanding Your Mortgage Calculation

How is my monthly payment calculated?

Your monthly mortgage payment is calculated using the following formula:

M = P[r(1 + r)^n]/[(1 + r)^n - 1]

Where:

  • M = Monthly payment
  • P = Principal loan amount
  • r = Monthly interest rate (annual rate divided by 12)
  • n = Total number of months (years × 12)

Components of a Mortgage Payment

Principal and Interest (P&I)

The base mortgage payment that pays down your loan and interest.

Property Taxes

Annual property taxes divided into monthly payments, typically held in escrow.

Homeowners Insurance

Required insurance coverage paid monthly through escrow.

Private Mortgage Insurance (PMI)

Required for conventional loans with less than 20% down payment.

Essential Mortgage Tips

Down Payment Strategies

  • Conventional loans typically require 5-20% down
  • FHA loans may accept as little as 3.5% down
  • VA loans often require no down payment
  • Larger down payments reduce monthly costs

Interest Rate Factors

  • Credit score impact
  • Market conditions
  • Loan term effects
  • Fixed vs. adjustable rates

Ways to Lower Your Payment

  • Improve your credit score
  • Make a larger down payment
  • Shop multiple lenders
  • Consider longer loan terms

Types of Mortgages

Conventional Loans

Traditional mortgages not backed by government agencies. Typically require good credit and larger down payments.

FHA Loans

Government-backed loans with lower down payment requirements and more flexible credit requirements.

VA Loans

Special mortgages for veterans and service members with no down payment requirement.

Jumbo Loans

Larger loans that exceed conventional loan limits, typically requiring excellent credit and larger down payments.

Frequently Asked Questions

What credit score do I need for a mortgage?

Credit score requirements vary by loan type: FHA loans may accept scores as low as 580, while conventional loans typically require 620 or higher. Better rates are usually available for scores above 740.

How much house can I afford?

A common rule is the 28/36 rule: your mortgage payment shouldn't exceed 28% of your gross monthly income, and total debt payments shouldn't exceed 36%.

Should I choose a 15-year or 30-year mortgage?

15-year mortgages offer lower interest rates and faster equity building but higher monthly payments. 30-year mortgages provide lower monthly payments but cost more in total interest.

What is mortgage insurance?

Mortgage insurance protects the lender if you default on your loan. It's typically required for conventional loans with less than 20% down payment and for all FHA loans.
